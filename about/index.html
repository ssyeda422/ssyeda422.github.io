About

Currently I am working at Boston Scientific as an IT Engineer under the Technology Leadership Development Program (TLDP), in which we complete four 6-month rotations on different teams within IT. This program has been an invaluable opportunity for me to understand the company more holistically. For example, I have been able to work within both our Commercial and Enterprise divisions, both of which provided unique perspectives and enabled me to put my technical background to use.

Apart from that, the program has really allowed me to cultivate my leadership and people skills. In just the past year, I feel that I have grown and learned so much in ways that may have seemed minor but gradually accumulated. Whether it be in mentorship, communication, proactivity, self-management, or any other realm, it has been incredibly rewarding to look back and see the progress I've made since I first started. As someone with a self-proclaimed growth mindset, I am excited to see how I can continue to grow from here!

Academically, I am highly interested in the intersection of technology and healthcare solutions. Working at Boston Scientific and UMass Medical School has truly shown me the ample opportunity that lies within the medical field and healthcare industry. In the long-term, my hope is to delve further into the research for this space and be at the forefront of its developments.

Outside of work, I love making artwork and designs, which you can check out on my artwork page! Living in suburban MA, I am also a big fan of outdoor walks and scenic trails (atleast 40% of my camera roll is comprised of the outdoor scenery category). I've been into nonfiction reading as of late (right now I am reading A History of the World in 100 Animals) so I'm always looking for new recommendations!
